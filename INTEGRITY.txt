Implement proactive database monitoring (performance, capacity, replication lag, blocking queries) with alerts configured to detect issues before they impact users. Target fewer than 2 incidents per year where root cause was a foreseeable and unmonitored risk. 1. INTEGRITY

Ensure all SQL Server production changes, approvals, access requests, and operational activities are performed with full compliance to bank policies, audit requirements, and risk controls.

Measures:

100% of production changes to have proper SNOW CHG / eMemo approval before implementation.
Zero unauthorized production changes.
Less than 3 cases of negligent approval or missing mandatory evidence.
100% of DBA activities such as backup, restore, patching, security, TDE/certificate, and audit-related tasks to have proper documentation and evidence.
Ensure timely escalation for any risk, control gap, or non-compliance issue.
2. CUSTOMERS FIRST

Maintain strong service focus for application teams, users, infra teams, and business stakeholders by tracking DBA tasks, demands, incidents, and service improvement items based on priority and business impact.

Measures:

Maintain a demand/task tracker for all SQL DBA requests, incidents, changes, and improvement items.
Review and update tracker at least weekly.
Achieve SLA compliance for incidents, service requests, and change activities.
Ensure critical application/database issues are communicated clearly and escalated on time.
Provide timely support for performance issues, blocking, backup/restore, SQL Agent jobs, Always On, SSRS, security, and audit requests.
3. PROACTIVE & INNOVATIVE

Proactively identify database risks and service improvement opportunities instead of working only in BAU/reactive mode. Improve SQL DBA operations through automation, monitoring, preventive checks, and better planning.

Measures:

Perform regular SQL health checks covering backup status, job failures, disk/capacity, Always On health, blocking, performance, security, and audit status.
Identify and track database risks early with action plans and target dates.
Deliver at least 3 improvement or automation initiatives during the year, such as backup validation automation, job failure alerting, patching tracker, health-check scripts, or audit evidence automation.
Reduce recurring incidents by identifying root cause and implementing permanent fixes.
Escalate major risks early with clear impact, options, and recommended actions.
4. SPEED & QUALITY

Deliver SQL DBA changes, projects, patching, upgrades, migrations, audit tasks, and service improvements with proper planning, timeline, quality checks, and post-implementation validation.

Measures:

100% of major DBA activities to have clear implementation plan, rollback plan, validation steps, and communication.
Maintain change success rate above 95%.
Less than 3 delivery delays caused by planning gaps.
Less than 16% monthly amber status across DBA deliverables, where applicable.
Complete SQL patching, backup, restore, DR, audit, TDE, certificate, and monitoring-related activities within agreed timelines.
Perform post-change validation and update stakeholders after implementation.
5. TEAM SMBC GROUP

Support other infrastructure, platform, application, security, and regional teams with a collaborative mindset to improve service stability and reduce escalations.

Measures:

Provide timely support to application, infra, network, security, and audit teams for SQL-related activities.
Participate actively in incident calls, change reviews, DR tests, patching, and project discussions.
Share SQL DBA knowledge and best practices with team members.
Create or update at least 3 SOP/runbook documents during the year.
Conduct at least 2 knowledge-sharing sessions on SQL DBA topics such as Always On, backup/restore, TDE, patching, monitoring, or performance troubleshooting.
Support cross-team escalations with ownership until closure.
